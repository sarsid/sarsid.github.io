Hello, now that you are here, I am obliged to tell you about myself. I am a senior undergraduate at the University of Waterloo finishing a joint major in Computer Science / Combinatorics and Optimisation (quite a mouthful).

I am interested in pursuing graduate studies in Theoretical Computer Science. I am especially interested in quantum and classical complexity and algorithms. You can look at my CV here.

In Fall 2019, I worked with Professor David Gosset on the complexity and scope of classical simulation of a family of stoquastic Hamiltonians. The result relies on the Monte Carlo algorithm of Jerrum and Sinclair on approximating the permanent. The idea is to examine the possibility of expanding the scope of techniques used by Bravyi and Gosset to a wider class of Hamiltonians and in particular, finding the limit of this technique. The project rests within the wider scope of fascinating research that utilizes methods in Quantum Monte Carlo and the matchgates formalism introduced by Valiant to develop classical simulation algorithms for quantum many-body systems. I wrote a program to enable searching through the space of possible graph gadgets that could correspond to new Hamiltonian terms. While we did not find any new graph gadgets this way, we produced strong evidence that any new graph gadgets probably feature a specialized structure. Furthermore, we proved that a limited but powerful class of graph gadgets only produce already simulable hamiltonians. We aim to expand this proof to all possible graph gadgets proving a negative yet surprising result.
